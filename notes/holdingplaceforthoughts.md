Authentic Chinese Food Blog

    Full discosure time, I am putting this together using information on the internet (mainly a very cool blog that is no longer up) and feedback from a couple friends. I have never been to China and am ignorant to most cultural and geographical aspects that define Chinese cuisine. Take what I say with a grain of salt.

    I took Manderin in college to fullfil a language credit requirement. I don't remeber much Manderin, but I did pick up an interest in Chinese food during and after the comnpletiuon of my final project "The different cuisines of the providences of China". My presentation, and my presenation of my presentation were terrible, but I like the idea of coming back and trying it again, while eating the food at the same time.

    I am going to try and cover the 8 major cuisines of China and their respective providences.
        Lu (Shandong)
            Flavors: Savory, umami‑forward, clean, lightly salty
            Ingredients: Wheat, corn, peanuts, scallions, garlic, vinegar, seafood
            Cooking styles: Clear braising, quick frying, roasting, soup and broth
        Chuan (Sichuan)
            Flavors: Spicy, numbing, aromatic
            Ingredients: Chili peppers, Sichuan peppercorns, garlic, ginger, fermented bean pastes
            Cooking styles: Stir‑frying, dry‑frying, braising, oil‑heavy preparations
        Yue (Cantonese)
            Flavors: Mild, fresh, lightly sweet, natural
            Ingredients: Pork, poultry, seafood, rice, seasonal vegetables
            Cooking styles: Steaming, quick stir‑frying, light braising, roasting
        Su (Jiangsu)
            Flavors: Subtle, balanced, gentle sweetness
            Ingredients: Freshwater fish, pork, bamboo shoots, river shrimp
            Cooking styles: Braising, stewing
        Min (Fujian)
            Flavors: Umami‑rich, lightly acidic, delicate
            Ingredients: Seafood, mushrooms, bamboo shoots, fermented sauces
            Cooking styles: Soups, slow simmering, steaming
        Hui (Anhui)
            Flavors: Earthy, savory, robust
            Ingredients: Wild herbs, mushrooms, tofu, pork
            Cooking styles: Slow braising, stewing, minimal oil
        Xiang (Hunan)
            Flavors: Spicy, smoky, tangy, bold
            Ingredients: Fresh chilies, smoked meats, garlic, shallots
            Cooking styles: Stir‑frying, smoking, steaming, pickling
        Zhe (Zhejiang)
            Flavors: Fresh, light, clean
            Ingredients: Seafood, bamboo shoots, leafy greens, rice wine
            Cooking styles: Gentle stir‑frying, steaming, quick braising
